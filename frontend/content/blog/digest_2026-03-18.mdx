Spring is heating up in the bowling world, and we're not just talking about the weather. This week brought us 50 new alleys on BAiO (mostly Michigan — they're having a moment), a rookie's perfect game that stole the show, and some well-deserved industry recognition.

## 🎳 On the Lanes

Rookie Brandon Bonta rolled a perfect 300 game to win the [PBA Players Championship 2026](https://www.bowlersmart.com/2026/02/22/rookie-brandon-bonta-rolls-300-to-win-pba-players-championship-2026/). Nothing quite like throwing strikes when it matters most — especially as a first-year pro. That's the kind of debut that gets people's attention.

## 📣 Industry News

The 2026 USBC Open Championships kicked off with opening ceremonies, and scoring pace is already picking up early in the tournament. Meanwhile, [USBC Hall of Famer Dorothy Fothergill passed away at age 80](https://bowl.com/news/usbc-hall-of-famer-dorothy-fothergill-dies-at-age-80) — a true legend whose contributions to the sport won't be forgotten.

On a brighter note, [Art Beasley was named 2026 USBC Youth Volunteer of the Year](https://bowl.com/news/art-beasley-named-2026-usbc-youth-volunteer-of-the-year), proving that the people who give back to youth bowling are still getting the recognition they deserve. The sport needs more volunteers like Art.

## 🛒 New Gear

Quiet week on the new releases front, though we did spot mentions of the Hammer Black Widow Tour V1 and Columbia 300 Piranha Solid making rounds. Sometimes the best gear weeks are the ones that let you focus on perfecting what you already have.

## 📍 New on BAiO

Huge week for new alleys — we added 50 spots to the platform! Michigan dominated with places like Hillcrest Lanes (Belding), Bellaire Lanes & Games (Bellaire), Brighton Bowl (Brighton), and dozens more. We also launched our [Best Bowling in San Antonio, TX](https://www.bowlingalleys.io) guide for anyone planning a Texas bowling road trip.

## 📊 Stat of the Week

**Mancelona Lanes in Mancelona, MI** takes the crown for top alley added this week. With a 4.6/5 Google rating backed by 97 reviews and a Bayesian weighted score of 4.498, this northern Michigan spot is clearly doing something right. When you're consistently hitting above our platform average of 4.3, that's not luck — that's delivering the goods every time someone walks through your doors.
