# Top Bowling Alleys in Boston, MA

Whether you're planning a family night out, league play, or a date with candlepin and flatbreads, the Boston area offers some of the best bowling in New England. Here are the top bowling alleys in and around Boston.

For a complete directory with pricing, hours, and venue details, visit our **[Boston City Guide](/best-bowling-in-boston)**.

## 1. [Central Park Lanes](https://bowlingalleys.io/venue/Sov6yk2MIXerpXCAGeIN)

**Location**: 10 Saratoga St, East Boston, MA 02128  
**Lanes**: 12 (candlepin)  
**Rating**: 4.7/5 (160 reviews)  
**Phone**: (617) 567-7073  
**Pricing**: $3/game, $15/hour, $1.50 shoe rental

Veteran family-run alley featuring New England-style candlepin bowling with shoe rentals. Paper-and-pencil scoring, BYOB, cash only. Twelve lanes and weekday-through-weekend hours for a no-frills, authentic candlepin experience.

## 2. [Sacco's Bowl Haven](https://bowlingalleys.io/venue/ZDVC6sYTRQhypBQyd3DF)

**Location**: 45 Day St, Somerville, MA 02144  
**Lanes**: 1 (candlepin)  
**Rating**: 4.6/5 (1,164 reviews)  
**Phone**: (617) 776-0552  
**Website**: [afbsaccos.com](http://afbsaccos.com/)  
**Pricing**: $39/hour, $4 shoe rental

Old-fashioned alley with candlepin bowling and a retro restaurant serving wood-fired flatbreads. Bar, food, and parties. A Somerville institution—very popular, so consider calling ahead.

## 3. [Urban Wild Boston](https://bowlingalleys.io/venue/uRyAkSGAdbTKzy4Jl21k)

**Location**: 100 Hood Park Dr, Boston, MA 02129  
**Lanes**: 1  
**Rating**: 4.6/5 (566 reviews)  
**Phone**: (617) 286-7300

Bowling plus food, bar, and live music in an upscale setting. Reservations recommended via their [booking site](https://secure.meriq.com/urbanwildboston/). Ideal for groups and a night out.

## Planning Your Visit

Each venue offers something different:

- **Central Park Lanes**: Best for authentic candlepin, BYOB, cash only, 12 lanes
- **Sacco's Bowl Haven**: Must-visit for flatbreads and candlepin; book ahead
- **Urban Wild Boston**: Upscale bowling with food, bar, and live music; reservations recommended
- **South Boston Candlepin**: Affordable hourly rate, leagues, and bar
- **Wakefield Bowladrome**: Quick candlepin fix; Fri–Sun only
- **American Flatbread Brighton**: Nine lanes plus wood-fired pizza; great for groups
- **Webster Timber Lanes**: Full amenities—pro shop, leagues, live music
- **Ron's Dedham**: Ice cream and candlepin; family-friendly
- **Olindy's Quincy**: Per-game pricing, billiards, darts, arcade
- **Hanson Bowladrome**: Weekend-only, golf simulator, community vibe

Whether you're after classic candlepin, pizza and bowling, or a reservable night out, the Boston area has a lane to match. For full details, hours, and maps, use our **[Boston City Guide](/best-bowling-in-boston)**.
